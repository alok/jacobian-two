# The smallest width-five `S6` near-miss is topologically dead

## Claim boundary

For a one-component `S6` branch, transposition monodromy forces affine
projection width at least five.  This note constructs and audits an exact
rational curve at equality with the smallest possible singular one-pair
degrees.  Its cusp, node, infinity, and genus data all fit the surviving
one-dicritical passport, but its affine complement group is `Z`.  It therefore
cannot carry connected six-sheet monodromy.

This excludes one degree-minimal target curve.  It does **not** prove that all
width-five curves are cyclic, eliminate the `S6` passport, construct a Keller
map, or solve `JC(2)`.

## 1. The width theorem

Let `B` be an irreducible reduced affine plane curve with polynomial
normalization

\[
  \nu:\mathbb A^1\longrightarrow B.
\]

Choose an affine-linear coordinate `ell` such that `ell restricted to B` is
finite, and put

\[
  w=\deg(\ell\circ\nu).
\]

Affine Zariski--van Kamp says that the complement of a generic projection
fiber—an affine line punctured at `w` points—surjects onto
`pi_1(A2-B)`.  The complement group is therefore generated by `w` geometric
meridians.

Now suppose a connected degree-`n` cover has generic ramification index two
only along `B`.  Every meridian maps to a transposition.  Regard those
transpositions as edges on the `n` sheets.  The generated-group orbits are
exactly the connected components of the edge graph.  Transitivity requires a
connected graph, hence

\[
  \boxed{w\ge n-1.} \tag{1.1}
\]

For the one-dicritical `S6` survivor, `w>=5`.  At equality the five supports
must form a spanning tree, so the five transpositions themselves generate all
of `S6`.

## 2. A degree-minimal geometric near-miss

Consider

\[
  \nu(t)=(P(t),Q(t))=(t^5+t^4,\;t^7+t^5). \tag{2.1}

\]

Its implicit equation is

\[
\begin{aligned}
b(p,q)={}&p^7-2p^6+9p^5q-7p^4q^2+2p^5-10p^4q\\
&+28p^3q^2-34p^2q^3+12pq^4-q^5-2q^4.
\end{aligned} \tag{2.2}
\]

Exact elimination verifies `Res_t(p-P(t),q-Q(t))=-b(p,q)`.  Moreover,

\[
  \gcd\left(
    \frac{P(t)-P(s)}{t-s},
    \frac{Q(t)-Q(s)}{t-s}
  \right)=1,
\]

so the parametrization is generically injective and is the normalization.

The width is exactly five.  Every affine-linear combination with nonzero
`Q` coefficient has degree seven; a nonconstant combination of `P` alone has
degree five.  If normalized degrees are `m<n`, then a singular one-pair
infinity germ has orders `(n-m,n)`, with `n-m>=2`.  Combining `m>=5` with the
smallest possible `n` gives `(m,n)=(5,7)`.  Thus (2.1) occupies the first
degree slot allowed by (1.1) and singular one-pair infinity.

## 3. Complete singularity census

The derivatives are

\[
  P'=t^3(5t+4),\qquad Q'=t^4(7t^2+5),
\]

and

\[
  \operatorname{Res}(5t+4,7t^2+5)=237.
\]

Hence `t=0` is the only ramified normalization point.  Its orders `(4,5)`
make it the allowed `T(4,5)` jump-three cusp, with delta six.

For off-diagonal collisions, exact elimination gives

\[
\operatorname{Res}_s\left(
 \frac{P(t)-P(s)}{t-s},
 \frac{Q(t)-Q(s)}{t-s}
\right)
=t^{12}(t^2+t+1)C_{10}(t), \tag{3.1}
\]

where

\[
\begin{aligned}
C_{10}(t)={}&t^{10}+t^9+2t^8+2t^7+3t^6+3t^5\\
&+6t^4-4t^3+2t^2-2t+2.
\end{aligned}
\]

The quadratic roots form one pair with image `(-1,-1)`.  The other ten roots
form five pairs whose distinct `p`-coordinates are the roots of

\[
  h(p)=p^5+2p^4+32p^3-152p^2-64p-16,
\]

because

\[
  \operatorname{Res}_t(C_{10}(t),p-P(t))=h(p)^2.
\]

They are distinct from one another and from the rational node:

\[
  \operatorname{Disc}(h)=2^{16}3^6 71^2 797,
  \qquad h(-1)=-135.
\]

Writing an unordered pair as `u=t+s`, `v=ts`, the five conjugate pairs obey

\[
  u^5+u^4-2u^3+4u^2-3u+1=0,
  \qquad v=\frac{u^2+1}{1-u}.
\]

The exact pair-distinctness and velocity-determinant resultants are nonzero:

\[
  2\cdot79,
  \qquad
  2^2\cdot5\cdot79\cdot797.
\]

The rational pair's velocity determinant is `-15`.  Thus all six collisions
are transverse two-preimage nodes.

The degree-seven closure has one point at infinity.  In the `Q=1` chart with
`u=1/t`,

\[
  X=\frac P Q=u^2\frac{1+u}{1+u^2},
  \qquad
  Z=\frac1Q=\frac{u^7}{1+u^2}.
\]

After straightening `X`, the branch has one pair `(2,7)` and delta three.  The
arithmetic genus is 15, and the exact accounting

\[
  \underbrace{6}_{T(4,5)}
  +\underbrace{6}_{\text{nodes}}
  +\underbrace{3}_{(2,7)\text{ at infinity}}
  =15
\]

shows that the census is complete.

## 4. The global complement collapses

Sage 10.8's unsimplified affine Zariski--van Kamp calculation gives five
geometric fiber meridians and the ten relators stored in
[`s6_width_five_curve.py`](../scripts/s6_width_five_curve.py).  Its exact
Tietze simplification sends every original meridian to the same generator and
has inverse sending that generator back to the first meridian:

\[
  \boxed{\pi_1(\mathbb A^2-B)\cong\mathbb Z.} \tag{4.1}
\]

The repo includes two independent replays:

- `sage tools/check_s6_width_five_curve.sage` regenerates the presentation,
  checks every signed relator, and checks the explicit isomorphism to the
  one-generator free group;
- `uv run python -m scripts.s6_width_five_curve` exhausts all
  `15^5=759375` assignments of the five geometric meridians to
  transpositions in `S6`.

Exactly 15 assignments satisfy all relators—one for each choice of a common
transposition.  Every image has order two, and none is transitive.  Therefore
the curve satisfies the local geometry and minimum-width test but fails the
decisive global complement-monodromy test.

## 5. What remains

The four-strand trace curve failed before reaching the width bound.  This
curve reaches equality and still fails because its five meridians collapse in
the complement group.  The next `S6` target must therefore be another
width-five curve with noncyclic complement, or a higher-width/multi-pair
branch, while retaining the forced cusp/collision budget and the Keller
compactification constraints.
